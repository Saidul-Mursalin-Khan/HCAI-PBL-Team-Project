Four ways to choose what to ask Random — just pick any stories (our baseline). Uncertainty — ask about the ones the computer is least sure of. Margin — ask when its top two guesses are neck-and-neck. Hybrid — mix random with uncertainty.

The lesson: asking smart questions beats asking random ones — but only up to a point. Being too clever early on can backfire, which is why the balanced hybrid approach is the safe all-round choice.

Show me the technical details

After each batch of expert queries we retrain the Step 3 deferral gate on just the queried points and evaluate on the held-out test set. At very small budgets, pure uncertainty/margin sampling can collapse to an "always defer" policy: it only ever queries the AI's hardest points, so the gate never sees an easy, AI-correct example to contrast against. The hybrid strategy keeps some random exploration to avoid this failure mode, at a small efficiency cost once budgets grow.
