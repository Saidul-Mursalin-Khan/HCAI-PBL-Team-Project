With both AI predictions and (simulated) expert labels available during training, we learn a small logistic-regression "gate" that decides, per example, whether the system should trust its own classifier or defer to the expert. The gate is trained to defer exactly when doing so would flip a wrong AI answer into a right one, and its decision threshold is calibrated on a held-out split to directly maximise combined system accuracy.

The learned system beats AI-only accuracy by deferring selectively and sparingly: it only asks the expert on the examples where the AI is least confident, and only when the calibrated threshold judges that querying is worth it. Note accuracy on the deferred subset is well below the expert's overall accuracy -- deferral is targeted at exactly the AI's weakest, hardest cases, where nobody (human or model) does great, but the expert still edges out the AI.
